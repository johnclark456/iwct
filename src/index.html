Peter Egan

I am delighted to be a Patron of the International Wildlife Coalition Trust.

I have been involved in Animal Welfare for over twenty years and it never ceases to amaze me how cruel humans can be when it comes to the pain inflicted on those, our voiceless companions, with whom we share our wonderful planet. None more so, I am sad to say, than when it comes to the appalling Dog Meat Trade. It is a trade which is frowned upon, in many cases, by the majority in those countries across Asia where it is practised.

It is thanks to the great work done by many charities in the region that attention is being brought to this vile trade, and moves are being made to try and stop it altogether. One such charity is IWCT. I'm glad to support it by being a patron.

Dogs Trust

Founded in 1891, Dogs Trust (formerly the National Canine Defence League) is the largest dog welfare charity in the UK. Their mission is to bring about the day when all dogs can enjoy a happy life, free from the threat of unnecessary destruction.

Charles Wartenberg

Chief Executive Officer

Charles Wartenberg has worked in animal welfare for over 40 years and has been involved with IWCT for over 20 years.

IWCT's involvement with trying to bring an end to the illegal dog meat trade in the Philippines began when Charles first visited the Philippines in November 1998. Arriving in a country without any grasp of the language he was determined to do something to help the plight of the poor dogs being savagely killed for the dinner table. In the early years Charles managed to turn Filipino journalists into investigators, looking into world of the illegal dog meat trade to identify the wicked perpetrators. Raids on illegal slaughterhouses, restaurants and markets selling dog meat, as well as seizures of vehicles transporting dogs for slaughter were successfully carried out and this has led to many arrests and convictions and of course the rescue of thousands of dogs.

In 2006 he was instrumental in the building and opening of the AKF Rescue_Centre in the Philippines. Charles splits his time between the Philippines and England and even brought one of the dogs he saved, a 4 month puppy rescued from a slaughterhouse just moments before death, home to live in the UK with him and his wife, they named him Sweet and he's now 14. When Charles first started his work in the Philippines approximately 500,000 dogs were believed to be being illegally slaughtered for meat and Charles is hoping that very soon he will be able to see the end of the trade altogether.
